Ca7riel & Paco amoroso
The uncrowned kings of Argentine music
Argentinian duo and rising stars of the trap scene, blending rap, funk, and electronic beats with explosive style.

Biography

beginnings

Catriel Guerreiro (Ca7riel) and Ulises Guerriero (Paco Amoroso) met during their childhood in Buenos Aires, sharing music workshops and school stages. From a very young age, they showed a strong artistic drive, taking part in various projects before forming their iconic duo.

Their first band, Astor y las Flores de Marte, blended rock, funk, and psychedelia, serving as the space where they developed their creativity and free-spirited style. That period became a sonic laboratory, marking the beginning of a limitless musical journey.

rise to fame

In 2018, they officially formed Ca7riel & Paco Amoroso, marking a turning point in the Argentinian urban scene. With a bold fusion of trap, funk, electronic, and rap, they released hits like “Piola”, “Jala Jala”, and “Ouke”, quickly establishing themselves as the new icons of Latin urban music.

Their strong visual identity, explosive stage presence, and charismatic energy made them generational symbols, blurring the line between underground and mainstream. Each performance reinforced their reputation as the shining gems of Argentinian trap, redefining the genre and reshaping the sound of a new wave of artists.

current times

Today, Ca7riel & Paco Amoroso stand as one of the most influential duos in contemporary Latin music. After performing at major festivals like Lollapalooza and appearing on NPR Music's Tiny Desk, they've taken their sound to a global audience.

Their current style blends experimental trap, electronic funk, and alternative pop, while keeping the irreverence, energy, and creative edge that define them. Constantly evolving, the duo continues to expand their musical universe through new albums, international collaborations, and an artistic vision that transcends genres and borders.

Albums

Papota

It's an ironic and introspective reflection on fame, success, and the pressures of the music industry, exploring how personal transformation comes with recognition.

Musically, it blends trap, funk, jazz-hop, electronic, and alternative pop, with a more organic sound — real instruments and live-band energy — compared to their previous releases.

The project is divided into two parts: the first revisits the vibe of their acclaimed NPR Tiny Desk session with reimagined performances, while the second introduces new songs touching on themes like impostor syndrome, friendship, and the ephemeral nature of success.

The title “PAPOTA” comes from Argentine slang for body-building supplements or “muscle boosts”, used metaphorically to critique the inflated, image-driven world of the music industry — where artists are “pumped up” for consumption.

Baño Maria

“Baño María” (2024) marks the duo's debut full-length album. Over 12 tracks, they dive into an eclectic mix of genres — from dembow, drum & bass, and electronic pop to R&B and funk carioca.

Thematically, the album plays with irony, hedonism, and satire — partying, fame, desire and identity all pass through a lens of wit and boundary-pushing visuals (including a jacuzzi premiere, surreal imagery and pop-culture nods).

Musically bold and stylistically free, Baño María encapsulates the duo's willingness to , embrace genre-fluidity, and challenge conventional urban-Latin formulas — all while keeping the dance-floor feel at its core.

Papota (short film)

Before the release of their latest album, Papota, Ca7riel and Paco Amoroso posted a teaser video on their channel with the same name as the album, featuring their new songs and telling a parody story about how to win a music award now that most of what matters in the music industry is physical appearance and controversy.
